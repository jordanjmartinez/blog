Hello World.

It is a beginners first step into programming. On its own, it does almost nothing, but it represents the foundation that everything else is built on.

The same is true in cybersecurity. Every professional starts as a beginner who has to learn how systems function, how they fail, and how they can be exploited. Breaking into the field can feel like a catch-22: cybersecurity is rarely treated as entry-level, but you need experience before anyone will give you a chance. The only way forward is to keep grinding: build home labs, break things, fix them, take notes, and show the work in public.

This post is my Hello World.

My first step into the world of cybercriminals, hacktivists, and nation-state actors.

The goal of this blog is to document the beginning of my cybersecurity journey: every mistake, every milestone, and the process of going from a complete beginner to someone with real experience.
